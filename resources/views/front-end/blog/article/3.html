5 Ways to Raise that Capital You Need

4 Comments 14 Likes

One truth about Nigerian entrepreneurs, especially startups, is the ability to raise capital for a business idea. The budding startup ecosystem particularly in Nigeria is so laced with innovative business ideas that it has the capability to take any business venture to the bank with the entrepreneur surely smiling after leaving the bank. That is however far from the case, because for any business startup to start, grow and thrive, the business critically needs three capitals.

  • The idea capital, which is the innovative idea required to make the business unique from and among other similar ventures.
  • The fund capital, helps to financially drive a business from conception, inception and finally on motion. The fund capital is so important, that hardly will the best business idea work without it.
  • The capacity capital is the technical and managerial competence to run the business startup.

Because the fund capital is crucial to a startup, it is therefore worthy to X-Ray how the budding startups can raise the needed fund for the venture. There are several ways to raise capital for business projects and startups particularly in Nigeria. This article takes a look at 5 sure ways that businesses, most importantly startups can raise that fund.

Personal Savings


Not the most recommended idea to raise business or startup capital, but one sure way to get there. This method will require the spending discipline of the entrepreneur. Using this method, the capitalist will ensure that un-necessary spending is avoided, standing-orders placed through the bank for a dedicated bank savings and a tight personal budget for the tenure of the savings. Personal savings is not a new form of raising capital; it has existed for a long time. The use of piggy bank is one form of personal saving used in time past. The best and most certain way to get money for your self is the one you give yourself, but the capitalist needs to remember that this method though it has it assurances, requires very strict and apt discipline.

Cooperative


As the saying goes, “A problem shared is half solved”. Belong to a cooperative with a common interest, where daily, weekly or monthly savings is carried out. The essence of this is to financially empower participating members. Each member of the cooperative contributes an agreed sum continually within a specified period. Members then take turns to get capital funds from the cooperative. This method ensures that every contributing member gets the needed capital to fund his or her venture, sometimes at zero percent interest. One drawback is an entrepreneur who has to urgently raise capital may have to wait for his or her turn

Microfinance Banks


As put by Wikipedia.com, the initial definition of a microfinance bank is the provision of microloans to poor entrepreneurs and small businesses lacking access to banking and related services. According to Wikipedia.com, The two main mechanisms for the delivery of financial services to such clients were: (1) relationship-based banking for individual entrepreneurs and small businesses; and (2) group-based models, where several entrepreneurs come together to apply for loans and other services as a group.

Microfinance banks give loans to empower businesses and startups meeting loan application requirements. Most Microfinance bank loans are tenure based; ranging from 3 months, 6 months to 12 months. Loans granted by Microfinance banks are usually short to medium term loans with little bureaucracy in paper works compared to commercial banks, however, interests on such loans can be as high as 24 percent

Angel Investor


Some businesses and startups require huge capital, while others may require aggregated funding. Whichever is the case; there are individuals who are ready to put their money on a workable idea or venture. These individuals are seeking for that ingenious idea. Entrepreneurs seeking for funds to drive a startup or existing business can use this alternative, but be ready to give up certain percentage of the business.
